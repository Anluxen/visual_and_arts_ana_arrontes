Artist Notebook

Cover

For doing the notebook cover I used a sponge, I stamped the paint of different colours on the paper. I also sticked with withe glue some rope pieces and a piece of tree bark in order to make more 3D relief. I stuck some flowerish cloth to add texture to the notebook. For doing the artist notebook I decided to use a notebook shape because it is something that is practical, so I can bring it everywhere.

First Page

I added a picture of the route that I did for my walking.

Second Page

I decided to experiment with colour because I took a picture of some yellowish flowers that attracted my attention, so I used different filters that I liked the result of and that made the picture different and interesting.

Third Page

In my first walk, I took a picture of two trees that the shape that they made together reminded me of a harp. I wanted to print the picture and stick some yarn to represent the strings of the harp.

Fourth Page

I sticked a picture of a fallen tree surrounded by nature and I got inspired by Bonnie Stahlecker’s notebook to do this kind of frame, cutting the cardboard, because she cutted in different shapes the pages to add 3D, so I did the same in my own way.

Fifth Page

I used the technique of frottage to represent the relief of the tree in the picture of the previous page. I used the yellow colour because it was brighter and I could see the relief better. Moreover, I made a frame for this page cutting black cardboard and sticking it. I used black colour because the contrast of the colours was bigger.

Sixth Page

I wanted to add a surprise and I cutted the cardboard in order to make a kind of “window” or “structure” that can be opened, and I added under the cardboard a piece of cloth that I found at home that is a tree and reminded me on my walk. Moreover, I sticked some leaves that I found on my walking, in order to add more texture and personality to the notebook.

Seventh Page

I included some pictures of the sky turning dark that I took during my first walk.

Eighth Page

I included two pictures of the same place but the first one is from the first walking and the second one from the second walking. In the first picture I could observe that the day was bright and in the second one was cloudy, so that is a clear difference of changes of colours. In addition, in the second picture I could observe that there was snow on the mountain and some yellow flowers had grown. I decided to cut this page in two because I thought that it would look good and feel like an album in which it is not necessary to observe both pictures at the same time, to add more dynamism.

Ninth Page

I included a picture of the tree bark because I liked the yellowish colour of the moss. I also cutted the cardboard with shapes inspiring in Bonnie Stahlecker’s artist notebook as on the fourth page.

Tenth Page

I represented the landscape that I saw glueing materials that I found on the walk, such as the grass or the plants, but for representing the clouds, I used cotton. I liked to represent the landscape with these materials because it adds 3D and texture. The cardboard was blue to represent the sky. For this page I got inspired by Cassandra Fernandez's notebook. Although I did not use paper and glue it on the cardboard, I used real materials and glued it on the cardboard.

Eleventh Page

I did a fast and simple drawing to represent the narrow river that I saw in my walk, the green drawing is a bush that I saw to the side of the river. I got inspired by Louis Boyd, as she painted what she saw, but I did not do a big and difficult drawing because I do not know how to do it.

Last Page

I got inspired by Louis Boyd and Susan Bowers at the same time. I got inspired by Louis Boyd by the structure of the page, as the page that has a picture of the path that I followed with yellow flowers, can unfold and inside I sticked four rectangular and narrow pictures of the landscapes of the walk inspired by Susan Bowers. I got inspired by her because she drew some drawings in this narrow and rectangular format and unified them, but instead of unifying them I sticked them together. Moreover, as I am not good at drawing and I prefered to take videos and pictures during my walk, I changed from drawing to taking pictures of the two walkins that I did. To conclude, I unified all the pages together with brown rope.
