Fina Miralles Exhibition

Who is Fina Miralles?

Fina Miralles (Sabadell, 1950) is one of the most significant artists in Spain from the seventies to the present. His work emerged in the dictatorial context of General Franco, in a hostile, limiting and castrating environment, in which censorship controlled all forms of expression under moral impositions, determined by the enormous power of the Catholic Church under the auspices of the regime.

The work of Fina Miralles breaks with the academicist proposals that were taught in the art schools of the time and with the established forms of behavior.

Fina Miralles spent seasons in South America, France and Italy, before settling permanently in Cadaqués in 1999. She was one of the first Catalan conceptual artists who, at the beginning of the seventies, carried out actions in nature, involving elements such as trees, earth, water and her own body. With great simplicity and forcefulness, these actions emphasize the dialogue between nature and artifice, and also the social and political critique in a context of the end of Francoism: totalitarianism, patriarchy and violence are the subject of analysis by the artist.

Paisatge.Mar (1979)

The artistic work that I liked the most about the Fina Miralles Exhibition is called Paisatge.Mar (1979). Paisatge. Mar is a container with water mixed with food coloring and fabric mounted on a wooden frame. This artistic work was created when Fina Miralles was rethinking her relation with painting in relation with the projects Paisatge i Doble horitzó.

They are works that without painting in the conventional way, show a great sensibility respect to the communicative possibilities of the basic elements and essentials of paintings, or respect the expressive and natural possibilities of colour. In the case of the use of basic elements at “Paisatge”, can be defined by a wooden frame, broken fabric and a natural element, for instance, stones, sand or farigola. There are two types of techniques of colour. On some occasions the artist lets the painting paint itself alone, without the intervention of the artist, as the case of Paisatge. Mar. On other occasions they are strips of paper that have been dyed by absorption and achieve a wide variety of shades of pink, brown, ochre and bluish.

I liked this artistic work because as I love the sea and going to the beach, the scenario of the artist's work made me feel peaceful and transported me to when I am staring at the sea. All the Paisatge artworks contributed to that feeling, as some of them reminded me of the sunset on the coast. Moreover I consider that it is innovative to paint the horizon of the sea with blue colourant and water, as they are common materials to find.

El primer bany de la sirena (1989)

The second artist work that I liked the most was El primer bany de la sirena. This artwork was created in 1989, oil on fabric. Although I did not find a lot of information about the significance of this artwork I could find the following statement about Fina Miralles’ meaning of the painting.

«L’aigua és la nostra primera Mare, i el primer Amor, de la Llum al reflex. Vull ser rierol, font, llacuna, ondulació marina o pluja fina sobre el llac. Si t’emmiralles en l’aigua, veuràs més enllà de la teva pròpia imatge, endinsa-t’hi i s’obrirà la porta del món invisible, on coneixement, inconscient i vivència estan units, i a dins teu trobaràs la font insecable de vida i amor.»
(Llibreta, Cadaqués, 2001) @Arxiu Fina Miralles Fundació Ars

I liked this artwork because I consider it “simple”, as it only uses bluish colours. The first time that I observed the painting I thought that it was women swimming because I had already seen some pictures about women swimming, but later on, when I read the title of the artwork and it said that it was a siren, I could see perfectly that it was a siren. In addition, although I did not know the meaning that wanted to transmit the artist with this painting, after reading her notes, I can not understand the meaning at all, but I believe that is interesting and with a lot of significance for the author, as she traveled a lot and while travelling found herself, as her identity was censored because of the dictatorship. This painting transmits calmness and a sense of reflex, as it is water.

Conclusion

To conclude I would like to mention that as the Fina Miralles exhibition is conceptual art, it was hard for me to interpret the artist's works. Also, I did not know anything about the artist, so it was harder to understand her feelings and what she wanted to transmit.

Furthermore, as I went to the exhibition with my friends from university, we were debating or telling our interpretations of the artist’s workstalking and that helped me to see different points of view and thinking.
